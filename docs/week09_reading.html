Exam structure

The midterm has three sections:

  • Section A — Concepts and code tracing (40%): Multiple choice and 'what is the output?' questions. Quiz questions from Weeks 1–8 repeat verbatim or with minor variation.
  • Section B — Fix the bug (30%): You receive a script with one or two bugs. Identify and correct them.
  • Section C — Short coding prompt (30%): Write a short function or script from a description.

Open notes — what this means

✓ You may use

Your own handwritten or printed notes · Your cheat sheet · Your assignment code from Weeks 1–8 · The course slides.

⚠ You may not use

The internet · AI tools of any kind · Communicating with classmates · Any resource not physically in front of you.

Study strategy

  • Rewatch the Week 5–8 video scripts — especially Week 5 (types, operators, input)
  • Redo any assignments where you lost points
  • Practice tracing accumulator loops on paper
  • Write three boundary tests for a function you wrote earlier in the semester
  • Read one traceback from your own code — bottom-up — and explain it aloud
  • Prepare a one-page cheat sheet with the most important code patterns

After the midterm

The second half of the course builds directly on what you just proved you know. Unit 3 takes the functions and data structures from Unit 2 and organises them into classes. Unit 4 connects those classes to a real database and a real user interface. Nothing from Units 1 and 2 was wasted.
